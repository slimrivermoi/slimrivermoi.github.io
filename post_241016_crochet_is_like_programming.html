Crochet is like programming

Commands, loops, execute, testing/debugging and the learning community.

Posted by Crochet Cat with ❤️ on Oct 16, 2024

I stumbled across this Rubyconf video from 2022 and I am really glad to have watched it. Tori spoked about her coding and crocheting experience and how the 2 topics share many similarities.

I do not know how to code with Ruby however what Tori spoke about highly resonates with me. The parts I loved the most (and i loved all of them!) is when she mentioned about the online community behind both crocheting and coding and how people are friendly to share and learn from one another. Another thing I love is when she talked about being a better teacher and mentor to others.

"Making (crochet) patterns and video tutorial helped me to learn how to better explain technical concepts to others. ....(and often time) through multiple modes of communciation! " -- Tori

I learned how to crochet about a year ago and I must say I'm still a beginner today. Even though my stitches are looking smoother and the desired outcomes are satisfactory, I find myself still having the "A-Ha!" moment whenever I am working on a new project.

Similar experience when I code as well. After completing a mini project, I often compared my codes with solution coldes or from other course participants. I always find it fascinating to learn how others approach the problem in a different manner to achieve the same outcome.

Whether it is about crocheting or coding, I would say that being patient with yourself in the learning journey and taking the time to practice and test your creation/coding out is really helpful. Try working on the same project again without peaking into the previous solutions: did your skill improve? Are you quicker in reaching the solution than before? Did you have a chance to further enhance your work? These are all great points for self-reflection.
